# BODY REFERENCE v2: Centerline + Mirror Plan

## Status

Proposed

## Goal

Define the next BODY REFERENCE direction for tumbler review so the app can:

1. capture the center vertical cylinder axis
2. capture a clean left-side body outline without handle contamination
3. scale the source image and SVG using tumbler lookup diameter
4. mirror the left outline across the centerline to generate the right side
5. revolve the mirrored body profile into a body-only GLB
6. keep lid outline data as a separate color-coded reference layer
7. keep handle outline data as a separate color-coded reference layer
8. use the handle outline for reference mapping and blocked or contaminated body regions
9. prevent lid or handle layers from contaminating BODY CUTOUT QA body geometry
10. keep BODY CUTOUT QA, HYBRID PREVIEW, and WRAP / EXPORT as separate preview intents

## Why v2

The current BODY REFERENCE flow already improved runtime truth, audit sidecars, BODY CUTOUT QA, and preview labeling. The remaining structural problem is that the body contour, lid contour, and handle contamination risk still live too close together in one review workflow.

BODY REFERENCE v2 should make the body shell pipeline explicit:

- centerline is the geometric axis of truth
- left body contour is the operator-approved contour of truth
- right body contour is generated by mirroring, not independently hand-traced by default
- lid and handle are reference-only layers unless a later feature explicitly promotes them into a separate geometry workflow

## Non-Goals

- Do not redesign BODY CUTOUT QA rules in this phase.
- Do not merge lid or handle layers into the body-only GLB.
- Do not bake silver rings or factory logos into the BODY CUTOUT QA GLB.
- Do not treat factory logos as user engraving artwork.
- Do not use ring or logo layers to determine the clean body outline.
- Do not let product appearance layers affect body-only QA pass or fail.
- Do not collapse HYBRID PREVIEW or WRAP / EXPORT into BODY CUTOUT QA.
- Do not add centerline or mirror behavior to flat products.
- Do not treat this plan as approval to rewrite reviewed GLB generation immediately.

## Source of Truth Model

### Geometric truth

- `centerline`: authoritative vertical axis for cylindrical symmetry
- `leftBodyOutline`: authoritative approved body contour
- `mirroredRightBodyOutline`: derived contour only
- `bodyProfile`: derived from `leftBodyOutline + centerline`
- `bodyOnlyGlb`: derived from `bodyProfile`

### Reference-only layers

- `lidOutlineLayer`: separate reference layer, never part of body-only QA geometry
- `handleOutlineLayer`: separate reference layer, never part of body-only QA geometry
- `blockedBodyRegions`: derived from handle contamination or keep-out analysis

### Product appearance reference layers

- `topSilverRingReference`: top finish-band reference, never part of body-only QA geometry
- `bottomSilverRingReference`: bottom finish-band reference, never part of body-only QA geometry
- `frontBrandLogoReference`: front factory-logo reference, never part of body-only QA geometry
- `backBrandLogoReference`: back factory-logo reference, never part of body-only QA geometry

These layers are appearance and orientation references, not body geometry truth.

- BODY CUTOUT QA must ignore them for body-only mesh generation.
- HYBRID PREVIEW, FULL / APPEARANCE preview, and WRAP / EXPORT may render them.
- WRAP / EXPORT may use them for orientation, keep-out hints, and placement context.

### Preview intent separation

- `body-cutout-qa`: body-only geometry proof
- `hybrid-preview`: body plus accessory or fallback preview allowed
- `wrap-export`: wrap math and export mapping proof

### UI behavior intent

- `body-left` should be visually dominant.
- `body-right-mirrored` should look derived, not hand-approved.
- `lid-reference` and `handle-reference` must look separate from body truth.

- front and back logo references must be distinguishable from each other at a glance.
- blocked regions must look alarming enough that BODY CUTOUT QA contamination is obvious.

## Product Appearance Reference Layers

These layers exist to preserve product appearance and orientation cues without contaminating body geometry truth.

### Finish bands

- `topSilverRingReference`
  - captures the top metallic finish band or silver rim
  - may define visual material transitions in preview
  - may later inform keep-out references for wrap/export review
- `bottomSilverRingReference`
  - captures the lower metallic band when present
  - may define visual material transitions or excluded finish regions

### Factory brand logos

- `frontBrandLogoReference`
  - identifies the factory front logo or badge
  - may anchor orientation and front-center reference
- `backBrandLogoReference`
  - identifies the factory back logo or badge
  - may anchor the rear orientation reference at approximately front + 180 degrees

### Authority rules

- These are reference and appearance layers only.
- They are not body outline truth.
- They must not be used to decide the clean body contour.
- BODY CUTOUT QA must ignore them when generating or validating body-only geometry.
- HYBRID PREVIEW, FULL / APPEARANCE preview, and WRAP / EXPORT may render them for visual context.
- WRAP / EXPORT may use them for orientation, keep-out hints, and visual placement context.

## Proposed Geometry Pipeline

### Phase A: capture and scale

1. Load source photo or traced SVG.
2. Capture or confirm `centerline`.
3. Resolve `lookupDiameterMm` from tumbler lookup.
4. Compute `mmPerPx` from diameter and source width assumptions.
5. Mark scale source as `lookup-diameter` when available.

### Phase B: isolate body truth

1. Capture clean left body contour only.
2. Exclude lid and handle shape from the body contour workflow.
3. Define blocked or contaminated body regions when the handle overlaps the silhouette.
4. Validate that the left contour spans the intended body shell top and bottom.

### Phase C: mirror and revolve

1. Mirror left contour across the centerline.
2. Generate a closed body profile from left plus mirrored right.
3. Revolve that body profile into a body-only mesh.
4. Write a body-only GLB and audit metadata.

### Phase D: reference mapping

1. Keep lid and handle outlines as separate layers.
2. Use handle outline to define front/back reference mapping and keep-out zones.
3. Keep wrap/export mapping tied to canonical diameter and wrap width, not to lid/handle silhouette.

## Preview Intent Rules

### BODY CUTOUT QA

- Must consume body-only geometry only.
- Must ignore lid and handle reference layers for body mesh generation.
- Must ignore finish-band and factory-logo appearance layers for body mesh generation.
- Must fail if contamination regions remain unresolved.
- Must never treat hybrid or accessory geometry as body proof.

### HYBRID PREVIEW

- May show body plus lid, ring, handle, or fallback accessories.
- May use reference and appearance layers for visual context.
- Must still label itself as not valid for body contour QA.

### WRAP / EXPORT

- Must show wrap diameter, wrap width, printable height, expected body dimensions, and scale source.
- Must stay separate from BODY CUTOUT QA proof.
- May rely on body-only geometry plus canonical wrap math.
- May render finish bands, front/back factory logos, and user engraving overlays together when labeled clearly.
- Must not claim body-only mesh proof if only wrap math is valid.

## Validation Rules

### Centerline validation

- centerline must exist
- centerline must be vertical within tolerance
- centerline must span the body shell top and bottom region
- centerline must lie between left outline and mirrored right outline

### Body-left validation

- left body outline must exist
- left outline must not cross centerline
- left outline must not include handle or lid segments
- left outline must cover the intended printable body shell height

### Mirror validation

- mirrored right outline must be generated from left outline, not edited independently by default
- left and mirrored right must remain symmetric relative to centerline within tolerance
- mirrored profile must produce a closed revolvable body path

### Scale validation

- lookup diameter should be present for tumbler BODY REFERENCE v2
- `mmPerPx` must be finite and positive
- resolved wrap width should remain consistent with `pi × diameter`
- expected body width and generated body bounds should stay within tolerance
- unknown scale source should warn, not silently pass

### Contamination validation

- handle-reference and lid-reference layers must not contribute vertices to body-only GLB
- finish-band and brand-logo appearance layers must not contribute vertices to body-only GLB
- blocked regions intersecting the approved body shell must warn or fail depending on severity
- BODY CUTOUT QA must fail if body mesh generation includes handle or lid contamination

## Proposed Contract Changes

Future `BodyGeometryContract` usage should remain split by authority:

- source truth: approved centerline plus left body outline
- reference truth: lid and handle layers
- appearance truth: finish-band and factory-logo layers
- generation truth: mirrored body profile and body-only GLB
- runtime truth: loaded-scene inspection
- preview truth: explicit intent label

### Phase 3: mirror-derived body shell

## Risks

- centerline capture may be unreliable on low-quality angled reference photos
- lookup diameter may be missing or wrong for unrecognized tumblers
- mirrored symmetry can hide real asymmetry if the source photo is perspective-distorted
- handle contamination may require manual blocked regions in some products
- operators may mistake reference layers for body truth unless colors and labels are very explicit
- operators may mistake factory logos or silver rings for user engraving if the viewer does not label them clearly

## Recommended Acceptance Criteria for v2

- Operator can capture a centerline and a left-only body contour.
- App can mirror the contour and generate a body-only GLB without handle or lid contamination.
- BODY CUTOUT QA consumes only body-only geometry.
- HYBRID PREVIEW can still show accessory context.
- WRAP / EXPORT clearly shows wrap math and stays separate from BODY CUTOUT QA.
- Audit and runtime contract metadata can explain whether the body mesh came from centerline-plus-mirror truth.
